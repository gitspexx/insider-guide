Listing Removal Policy

Last updated: 2026-04-30 · Version: 1.0.0

Effective date: 2026-04-30
Version: 1.0.0

This Policy explains how a business can be removed from Insider Guide if it does not wish to appear. It is part of, and incorporated by reference into, the Insider Guide Terms of Service.

What this is for

Insider Guide includes non-paying listings of businesses that Creators have curated based on first-hand experience or public information. We rely on the legitimate interest of operating a curated travel directory (GDPR Art. 6(1)(f)) — but we balance that against the legitimate interests of any business that does not wish to be listed.

If you operate a listed business and do not want to appear on Insider Guide, request removal below. No fee, no questions asked, and we honor it within 14 calendar days.

How to request removal

Email legal@insiderguide.co with subject line Listing Removal — [Business name, City] and include:

1. The exact business name as listed (or a URL to the listing on insiderguide.co);
2. Your name and your role at the business (owner, manager, director, authorized agent);
3. A short statement that you are authorized to act on behalf of the business;
4. The business address, city, country;
5. (Optional) Any specific listing element you want addressed first (e.g. a photo).

You do not need to give us a reason.

What we do

Within 14 calendar days of receipt of a substantively complete request, we will, at our option:

(a) Remove the listing entirely from Insider Guide and delete back-end records on a 30-day deletion cycle (with the exceptions noted under "Records we retain" below); or

(b) Anonymize the listing — strip business name, address, photos, and any uniquely identifying detail — keeping only de-identified statistics that no longer identify the business.

We will email you a confirmation when the action is complete.

What we cannot do

  • We cannot remove the listing from third-party search engines, social media, or other platforms — only from Insider Guide. To remove yourself from Google Maps, request via Google Business Profile; from TripAdvisor, request via TripAdvisor; etc.
  • We cannot remove general factual statements made by Creators about the category or destination that do not specifically identify your business.
  • We cannot remove content covered by an active legal hold, court order, or DMCA / counter-notice process.
  • We cannot retroactively undo a Creator's first-hand opinion having been published; we can only remove the listing.

Re-adding

If you change your mind, email the same address with subject Listing Re-add Request. We may, in our sole discretion, accept or decline (e.g. we may decline if a Creator has since moved on from the destination and there is no longer fresh first-hand content).

Records we retain

After removal, we retain (under legal-hold and operational obligations) only:

  • the fact that you requested removal, the date of the request, and the date of completion;
  • a checksum of the removed content for audit;
  • back-end records covered by tax / regulatory retention rules (e.g. if you were ever a Brand Partner who paid fees).

These records are not publicly visible and are retained per our Data Retention Policy.

Right of publicity / GDPR Art. 17 / California §3344

A removal under this Policy is, by design, broader than what GDPR Art. 17 ("right to be forgotten") or California §3344 (right of publicity) would strictly require for a business. We do not require you to invoke any specific legal basis. We honor the request.

If you instead prefer to invoke a specific legal right (so the request is recorded on that basis for audit / regulator-facing reasons), say so in your email and we will record it accordingly.

DMCA

If your concern is copyright infringement of a photo or text in the listing, this Policy is not the right channel. Use the DMCA Policy instead — that gives you a stronger statutory remedy.

Bad-faith / weaponized removals

If a person not authorized to act on behalf of a business submits a removal request to harm that business or a competitor, that is fraud. We may:

  • decline the request and require additional verification;
  • restore the listing if removal was based on a misrepresentation;
  • pursue any remedy at law against the misrepresenting party.

Audit trail

Each removal request is logged with timestamp, requester email, requester IP, listing-URL, action taken, and completion date. Logs are retained indefinitely under legal hold.
